Basic Basis

The Basis are a set of linearly independent vectors which define some space. Green and Red depict our two basis vectors. They are linearlly independent because they can only stretch and shrink on their axis. Purple describes the vector that the two basis vectors can reach. We can see that the purple vector can hit every point on this space.

Non Orthogonal Basis

Our vectors don't need to be orthogonal (cross at 90 deg). Red (v1) and Green(v2) still depict our two basis vectors and we see that they are still linearly independent because I can't the red vector by stretching or shrinking green and vice versa. We can also see that we can define purple with these two basis vectors, and likewise, we can hit each grey dot by stretching the combination of red and green.

Kernel

The Kernel of a linear transformation (red points) is the space that goes to 0 during the linear transformation.
When no dimensionality reduction happens eg. 2d to 1d, the only point in the kernel will be 0. 0 is always in the kernel.

Image

The Image of a linear transformation (red points) is the space (span) which the linear transformation maps to.
